Research

Research Statement

My research focuses on understanding how machine learning systems behave in real-world environments, particularly under uncertainty, noisy data, and practical deployment constraints. I am interested in improving model reliability, robustness, and evaluation methodologies for applied artificial intelligence systems.

A key focus of my work is bridging the gap between model development and real-world performance, especially in settings where data is imperfect, assumptions are violated, and decisions have meaningful consequences.

Research Interests

  • Machine Learning Systems
  • Model Evaluation
  • Robustness and Reliability
  • Uncertainty in AI systems
  • Human-centered AI
  • Applied AI in Health and Education

Selected Projects

AI Systems for Health Decision Support

Developed and evaluated machine learning and language model–based systems for health-related applications, focusing on system reliability, robustness, and behavior under real-world constraints.

Tech stack: Python, machine learning pipelines, evaluation frameworks.

Machine Learning for Educational Systems

Built AI-assisted educational systems and analyzed their performance, consistency, and usability under realistic deployment conditions. Focus was placed on experimental evaluation and system reliability.

Tech stack: Python, LLM-based systems, experimental evaluation.

Predictive Modeling for Health and Social Data

Developed classification and prediction models using structured datasets, focusing on preprocessing, feature engineering, and performance evaluation.

Tech stack: Python, scikit-learn, data analysis, model evaluation.
