Salome Monthe Chemiat – Machine Learning Researcher

I am a machine learning researcher at Strathmore University working on artificial intelligence systems, model evaluation, reliability, and data-driven applications in real-world environments. My research spans health, education, and decision-support systems, with a focus on how machine learning models behave under practical constraints and how they can be designed, tested, and evaluated responsibly.

Research Interests: Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, Model Evaluation, System Reliability, Human-Centered AI, Data-Driven Systems, and Applied AI in Health and Education.

Short Bio

I hold an MSc in Information Technology from Strathmore University, where my research focused on the development and evaluation of AI systems for health and educational applications. My work combines machine learning, language technologies, and experimental evaluation to study system behavior in real-world settings.

My broader research interests include machine learning, model robustness, system reliability, and the application of artificial intelligence to societal challenges. I am particularly interested in understanding how intelligent systems can be evaluated and improved for safe and effective deployment.

Research Snapshot

My research focuses on understanding how machine learning systems behave in real-world environments, particularly under uncertainty, noisy data, and practical deployment constraints.

I am interested in model evaluation, robustness, reliability, and the interaction between data, algorithms, and human decision-making in applied AI systems.
