About

My work sits at the intersection of Natural Language Processing, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), and Neurosymbolic AI. I investigate how retrieval systems can fail in subtle but critical ways—particularly when semantic similarity does not align with real-world usefulness.

I am especially interested in high-stakes applications such as healthcare, where incorrect or misleading outputs can have serious consequences. My research focuses on failure-mode analysis, robustness, and the integration of symbolic reasoning to improve system reliability.
